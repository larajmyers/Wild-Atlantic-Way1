Food and Culture

Food

The food on the Wild Atlantic Way highlights fresh seafood, local meats, and traditional Irish recipes. Along the coast, you can enjoy oysters, fish, and hearty stews made with ingredients from nearby farms and the sea. It’s a great place to taste authentic Irish flavors while taking in beautiful ocean views.

Eala Bhán Restaurant

Sligo Town, Co. Sligo

Eala Bhán Restaurant offers a fine dining experience. The décor at Eala Bhán has an urban, brasserie and bistro feel.

The Irish Soda Bread Way

Westport, Co. Mayo

Book in with the Irish Soda Bread Way, an authentic food experience based in the beautiful heritage town of Westport in County Mayo.

Wild Honey Inn

Lisdoonvarna, Co Clare

Wild Honey Inn is a family run inn located in the Burren region of County Clare, in the town of Lisdoonvarna.

The Rusty Mackerel

Teileann, CO Donegal

The Rusty Mackerel is a traditional Irish pub offering fine ales and wine in a friendly environment.

Quinlan's Seafood Bar

Killarney, Co. Kerry

Quinlan's Seafood Bar, located in the centre of Killarney town, will tempt diners with fresh seasonal fish and seafood in a casual elegant setting

Matt Molloy

Traditional Irish music pub located in the centre of Westport.

Culture

The culture along the Wild Atlantic Way is rich and lively, deeply rooted in Irish traditions. From traditional music sessions in local pubs to centuries-old crafts and festivals, the region celebrates its heritage with pride. Visitors can experience warm hospitality, storytelling, and a strong connection to the land and sea that shape everyday life.
