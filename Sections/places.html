Wild Atlantic Way

Popular Places

The Wild Atlantic Way has many places that you can visit. The Wild Atlantic way showcases and displays a range of beautiful landscapes and scenery along the west coast of Ireland. These places include The Cliffs of Moher, Keem Bay, Old Head Of Kinsale and Malin Head. All these places have a lot of nature and greenery exposed.

The Cliffs of Moher

The Cliffs of Moher are located in County Clare and are one of Ireland's most famous landmarks. They were founded on the southwestern edge of the burren region. They are home to thousands of diffrent types of seabirds including puffins and razorbills. They also include amazing views of other landmarks including the Galway Bay and the Aran Islands.

Keem Bay

Keem Bay is a coastal scenic bay located in Mayo. Located in County Mayo, Ireland, on the western side of Achill Island, Keem Bay is a remote haven renowned for its crescent-shaped sandy beach and glistening turquoise waters. The bay has breathtaking views and a tranquil haven from the hustle and bustle of daily life, surrounded by majestic cliffs and rolling green hills. Originally used for conventional fishing, it is now a well-liked location for swimming, hiking, and taking pictures.

Malin Head

The Old Head of Kinsale is a dramatic headland located on the southern coast of County Cork, Ireland, about 25 kilometers from Cork City. Stretching into the Atlantic Ocean, it is renowned for its soaring sea cliffs and breathtaking coastal views. The area is steeped in history, most notably as the nearest land point to the 1915 sinking of the *Lusitania*. Today, it is home to a historic lighthouse and a world-class golf course, making it a destination of both natural beauty and cultural significance.
